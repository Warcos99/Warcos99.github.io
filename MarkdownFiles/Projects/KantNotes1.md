### Background: Who was Kant

![Kant](/Assets/Images/KantNotes1/Kant.jpg)

Information from [britannica.com](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Immanuel-Kant)
- April 22, 1724 - Feb 12 1804
- German
- Enlightenment Era
- Lived in Remote Province
- Really liked the Latin Naturalist Poet Lucretius
- Enrolled in university (1740) in Theology, and did some preaching
- was into math and physics.  He began reading the work of Sir Isaac Newton.
- His first book (1746, Thoughts on the True Estimation of Living Forces) was about kinetic forces.
- Then, since his dad died he stopped academia to pursue more fiscally responsible life choices
- Became a tutor for a while
- in 1755, he finished school, became a lecturer and did alot of math and physics.
- 1756 - Monodologia Physica - Contrasted Newtonian thinking with philosophy in German universities.  
- He became really critical of Leibnizian Rationalism.  Big Newton stan
    - 1764 - An Inquiry into the Distinctness of the Fundamental Principles of Natural Theology and Morals
    - 1763 - An attempt to introduce the Conception of Negative Quantities into Philosophy
    - 1766 - Dreams of a Spirit-Seer, Illustrated by Dreams of Metaphysics
- Finally became professor in 1770, chair of logic and metaphysics
- in this period is when he produced his famous critiques.
- 1781 - Critique of Pure Reason

So it seems like alot of the context here hinges on understanding a little bit about Leibnizian Rationalism that he spent so much time criticizing.

### Rationalism

This next section is from [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rationalism)

"In philosophy, rationalism is the epistemological view that "Regards reason as the chief source and test of knowledge"" - First sentence in wikipedia.

So this is guys like Descartes, Spinoza, and Leibniz.  It seems like they are really really big on Knowledge, truth, and the ability to logic through things. 

They were so big on fundamental truths and principles that they found empirical proofs and physical evidence unnecessary.

"Reason has precedence over other ways of acquiring knowledge" and "Reason is the unique path to knowledge"

Politically, Rationalism emphasized utilitarianism, secularism, rationality, and deontology.  It became socially conflated with atheism.

compared to empiricism, All ideas com to us a posteriori (through experience)
but the rationalist holds knowledge is a priori (through the use of logic, independent of experience)

## 1. Of the difference between Pure and Empirical Knowledge

It is clear that knowledge in some part comes from senses (Empirical Knowledge)
(Senses) -> (Brain) -> (Knowledge) 
We call this knowledge *a posteriori*. But there is other knowledge out there, let's call it *a priori*. *a priori* knowledge is defined as knowledge that is known independently of the senses.  It is important to note that at this point he have not fully defined  *a priori* knowledge yet, we are just painting a picture to distinguish these two types of knowledge.

 "Every change has a cause" is an example of an impure *a priori* knowledge. I guess this is because as "change" as a concept is only known *a posteriori*.
