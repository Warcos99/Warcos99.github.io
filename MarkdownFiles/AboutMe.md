You will find I am horrendously inconsistent at being online.  All those links posted above will contain some part of me, but they are all also not a good place to regularly hear from me. If for god-forsaken reason you still wish to see what I am up to, this website is probably the place to be.

Hello, my name is Marcos, the domain of this website is Warcos because I found the "Wario" joke funny enough to spend 7 dollars a year on it.  I am a library worker in Northeastern Colorado. I have a bachelors degree in mathematical physics, which is simultaneously the most impressive thing about me, and the least useful endeavor I have partaken in (when you consider it from a career mentality).  I am one of the few people working in the admin team for the BlackBox Arts Collective, Someday that will be a big deal, but for now that just means a second part time job that I do entirely as a volunteer.  My fingerprints on the collective can be seen on the Regular Zine we publish "Burning Down Neverland" as the head "zine rat", One of the lead curators and designers of the gallery space we have, and the composer of the most eloquent newsletters you have ever seen.

My politics skew to the radical left, but all that "radical left" really means is that I think billionaires are uncool, and that they should be taxed.  As I keep updating this site with my essays, I think my politics will become more and more clear.  Generally I spend a lot of time thinking simultaneously about the moment we live in, and all the context behind it.  If we were to discuss a particular topic, I would be just as interested in discussing the underlying forces behind that topic.  For example, I will sit here and discuss with you the trend of young people becoming more polarized in either political direction, but to do so you must indulge me in a discussion of the nature people to skew to more direct and authoritarian politics when faced with economic adversity. Why is that? Is that good or bad? Is that drive what is secretly under the hood of some of histories greatest revolutions? What does that say about us as a people?    Behind any good question lies a sea of context that often goes under-explored.

My hobbies include tinkering with electronics, learning computer sciences, writing, painting, making music, bookbinding, finding something online i want and learning how to make it instead of buying it, and most importantly: freaking out about my ego as I try to understand why I am driven to post my thoughts in a public space like this.
