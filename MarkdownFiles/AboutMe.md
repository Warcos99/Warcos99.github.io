# About Me 

![cat](/Assets/Images/cat.jpg)

My name is Juan Marcos Aviña Jr. I am really just a library worker, but on my downtime, I engage in art, science, philosophy, politics, and whatever the hell else I want.  I have come to a place in my life where I no longer feel constrained to fit within the walls of expectations that are built for the aesthetic sensibilities of the public will. I believe I have reached this point through a non-trivial amount of privilege. I never had and probably never will have much money, rest assured every paycheck I get is well calculated, and every month is a crap shoot whether we are eating rice and beans or just beans.  But what I have been lucky enough to be a part of that others struggle to find is community. Now I am lucky enough to be surrounded by wonderful artists and thinkers that challenge me every single day, before it was my hometown church that looked after each other as a big extended family. 

I went to school to study mathematical physics, got my bachelors degree at UNC, and proceeded to work in a warehouse for several years instead. Eventually landed at the library system and have been here since.  I've been able to meditate on my journey and feelings about *work*, *meaning*, and how it all ties to the capitalist ecosystem in which we exist.  I have found meaning in life that is separate from career aspirations, and I have learned to believe that the only thing that truly matters is what you decide matters. I will spare you the anti-capitalist diatribe because I suspect you've heard it all before, we should find meaning and joy in our human connections and hobbies, not in the furthering of company goals, yadda yadda. But again, I think I have reached this point of enlightenment because I somehow landed in a job that doesn't make me want to kill myself every morning, I don't underestimate how hard and how soul crushing it can be to try to be free in a world where you are just not.  
   
On a local level, I consider myself an anarchist, though I am unsure if that would work at a national level, so I accept the necessary evils of government.  Philosophically, I align myself alot with the existentialists, like Camus and Sartre, though I've only read their fiction, attempts at reading their philosophical texts have left me confused as hell.  

I think that's the nuts and bolts of it all.  My name is Marcos, you can call me Warcos, and I hope my commonplace notes here serve you some sort of function!
