# Existential Goo, or "I think I am a Ghost"

I think I am a ghost.

I realize this is a common thing to say, but I really do mean it! Many non-ghosts like to identify as ghosts to live out a fantasy of solitude or escapism. By claiming to be a ghost, you might think that this is what I am doing. But no, I think I am a ghost, a real ghost. As in, I must have died recently. I cannot remember dying, I woke up in my bed like any other day except that I was a ghost. I must have died in my sleep.

I suspect that I am a ghost, because upon my awakening, I felt a deep lightheadedness forcing my senses to slow, while at the same time elevating my mood. This on it’s own may not convince you of my newfound ectoplasmic nature, but I assure you it was wild. I would move my hand in front of me at a reasonable speed, mimicking the swatting of a fly, but my eyes would perceive it moving much, much slower. The sunlight filtering through the window curtains would diffract through the cracks on the window glass causing it to split into a cavalcade of colors proudly marching into my walls and ceiling, coloring my perception of the situation. I couldn’t help but giggle.

And here is the real kicker, it turns out I was also intangible! I started sinking deeper into my bed, and at first I thought that was because the bed was particularly fluffy. But no, after some seconds I realized that the sinking was due to my body passing through the body of the bed. Before I realized it, I was on the floor underneath the bed, and then I was halfway through the floor through several inches of construction material and insulation (and a couple rats).

I go through the floor and land on Miss Mason’s coffee table. I only had a couple seconds to process what was happening, so I could not tell you what was going on in her mind, but behind me the television showed a woman in an office had just slapped a man across the cheek, and both parties looked shocked. In fact, behind me, Miss Mason was also in shock. I could not tell you if she was shocked at my sudden presence in front of her television or if it was due to the climactic scene of the telenovela.

But after a dozens of seconds in my brain (what must have been a mere moment in reality), I make it through the floor again and fall into a dining table in what must have been an empty first floor apartment. At this point I felt a sudden physical freedom about me, specifically in my groin area. Looking down at my body I realized my clothes had fallen off me, were they on Miss Mason’s coffee table? Oh god, that’s embarrassing. My giggle turns into a nervous one. At least no one is around to see my exposed organs.

Then I fell through the tangible table and through the tangible floor.

What followed was hours of falling through the ground, surrounded by a pitch black darkness, as no sunlight could ever hope to penetrate this deep underground.

I continue to giggle.

---

How many hours has it been? I’ve been falling for an eternity now. The taste on my tongue was terrible at first, it was acidic and … chemically? It tasted what I imagine battery acid would probably taste like.

But now the ground just tastes like really cheap and angry coffee.

I laugh.

---

I’m not sure when this happened, but I’m even more naked now. As in, all my tangible bits have begun falling off my body.

At first it was my skin, it was pitch black so I’m not sure how I could tell, because it didn’t really hurt.

Actually I could not feel anything at all. I just knew, somehow, that I was a pile of skin-less muscles now. Without much pomp or circumstance, I had lost my muscles and organs too. The only reason I noticed anything at that point is because I could see when my eyes were pulled away. It was a sudden stretching of darkness around me followed by emptiness.

The pitch black darkness that resulted from being deep underground had a warm-ness that I could perceive in my soul. When my eyes left my body and all I could see was the nothing-ness, everything suddenly became much colder. It was at this moment that I also lost the ability to taste the ground. The only thing I had left were the memories of the taste and look of the underground.

My vestibular system had left me alongside all my other senses, so I lost the ability to feel the g-forces on my soul and as far as I could tell I was just a brain trapped in an eternity of nothingness. I felt no taste, no sight, no touch, not even momentum.

At some point I must have lost my bones too. I could not tell you when it happened, I just know that I did. Something deep inside my soul tells me so.

It’s all gone now, and only I can wonder what people will think of me.

I think that when I get back home I will have to drape a blanket over myself just so that other people could perceive me.

---

I’ve been wondering something. I understand that having an intangible body means falling through floors or going through walls. But, how is it exactly that I am falling? At this point I am a massless being, so what force does gravity exert over me? I shouldn’t be falling!

I should be able to float up as I wish, right?

...

I try to stop falling, but I realize I cannot tell if I succeed. But something in my gut says I fail to stop falling.

I moan.

---

The mighty photon is still subject to gravity’s rule. Light, for as massless as it may be, still cannot escape the awesome power of gravity. Gravity is so powerful that it can orthogonally rotate the nature of the dimensions of time and space so that space behaves like time and time behaves like space. We are talking about time and space!

The formation of our entire physical existence relies on the force of gravity pulling masses together and forming celestial objects. The spherical nature of the planets and stars, the orbital nature of the mechanics of these Godly beings, it’s all because of gravity.

There are aspects of gravity that are inescapable and persistent no matter who you are. There are also aspects of gravity that are mysterious and impossible. Questions of gravitons, dark-matter, the shape of the universe, the nature of spacetime, gravitational waves, geodesics, wormholes, and chaos are all questions of gravity.

It even affects the metaphysical, love and hate can be framed through the lens of gravity. Sociology, theology, psychology, these can all be framed through concepts of gravitational nodes that we orbit around as we attempt to understand life. The death-drive that fuels all human endeavors, it’s just a particle on a chaotic orbit around a node, where the node is Death, the particle is me, and the orbit is my life. For as seemingly unique and indescribable as my life might have felt, it was probably very describable with a couple gravitational equations, given we framed the data correctly.

Now that I have actually died, gravity persists in its mysterious and incredible power. Pulling me down into the center of the earth.

If there is a God, then surely, it is either Gravity, or it too is subject to Gravity. And I was a fool to think that I could overcome it.

I cry.
