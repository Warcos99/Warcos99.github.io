# The Emperor has no clothes

Until we are able to tell the emperor he has no clothes, he will continue to run around like a naked lunatic. So I urge you to loudly express the things you see to be true, no matter how obvious they may sound.

Here are my observations:

1. We are not heading towards an authoritarian surveillance state. No, my friends, we are already there. In our stores, phones, streets, and even our own homes, we are surrounded by systems like Flock Cameras that can give the state an exact log of where we are, who we talk to, and what we buy.

2. Refusal to participate in their systems will be taken as violent action by those with the true monopoly on violence. You can see that by how violent ICE is reacting to the friction they receive. You can also see that by looking at the short history of the Zapatista movement in Mexico.

3. American hegemony on the world stage is increasingly more fragile and uncertain. Friend and foe alike are doing their best create systems by which they can stop relying on the American dollar and military.
   
   In times of uncertainty, there is war.

   In times of war, empires fall.

   Regardless of what happens in the near future, it is clear we are living in historic times, and with that comes our ability to make our voices louder than ever. Now is not the time to give up!
   
4. “But how can any of this change?” I hear you ask. We are constantly presented with two options: voting and protesting. Both kind of work and both kind of don’t.

   Voting in local elections can have a significant effect in your local communities. For Example, Baltimore’s Mayor, Brandon Scott has consistently been reducing crime and incarcerations in the area through radical systems that puts more emphasis on community engagement than on policing.

   This is real and can happen here.

   Protesting has many functions, but mainly it lets you and everyone else know you are not alone. It provides a safe space for “dangerous” and “radical” ideas to fester. When everyone can believe the same thing, it becomes harder for that truth to be taken away. Evil on behalf of those in power happens with the implicit permission of those they rule over.

5. Both are super important, especially now more than ever. But clearly they are not enough, otherwise we would not be in this mess. So then, how does real change happen? I don’t know. Historically it has happened in many different ways, sometimes violence is necessary, sometimes it’s not. I don’t think change is formulaic, and enacting meaningful change is complex. Personally, I believe that forming a strong community is an important part of the process, but frankly, I don’t know. What I do know, is that enacting change will require a lot of imagination and active problem solving. Who better to figure this out than a community of artists?
6. Debate is so fucking useless. There is real value in conversation and humanizing yourself to your neighbor. But debate is not that. Debate has been perverted into something akin to entertainment slop. The goal of debate is not to educate or to expand your frame of reference. The goal of debate is to win. Don’t fall for their traps, don’t waste your time.

7. On all sides of the political spectrum, affordability, the Epstein files, the murders of American citizens by ICE, and the war in Iran, all these things are deeply deeply unpopular. Don’t let the current administration (and all others to follow) slip by without answering for this. Use this in your propaganda. (Also make Propaganda)

   Remember, It’s not left vs right. It’s top vs bottom. and the ultra rich elite have spent enormous amounts of money to fuel this fake fight between us in the middle to lower classes so we don’t pay attention to them.

   Your enemy is not the crazy racist uncle that won’t shut up about Trump. Your enemy is multi billionaire that is profiteering of the war in Iran, and who lobbied to cut your healthcare and social welfare programs because it meant more profit to them.
   
8. Build your community, it gives you safety. If you can’t afford to eat, come find me, I make a mean Beans and Rice. But in the foreseeable future, things will get harder before they get easier. Having a support network that can keep everyone within it fed, safe, and emotionally stable is the super power that comes with “community”.

9. Be safe, like I mentioned earlier we are in a surveillance state. If they decide they don’t like you, they will make you a prisoner, not a martyr. I don’t say this to make you scared, but rather to make you responsible. Use secure encrypted communication when possible. Avoid the use of self-surveillance gadgets like Alexa or Ring doorbells. Reduce your online footprint as much as you can. For some this is easy, for others it may take some baby steps. But removing yourself from the infrastructure that google, meta, and other similar companies have built to keep you addicted, is very do-able (even without having to sacrifice a smart phone). You are more useful to the cause out there, next to me, making a change, than you are locked away.

10. Lastly, Fuck ICE, Fuck Trump and his Cronies, Fuck all this fascist bullshit, and if being an antifascist puts me on a terrorist list, at least i can sleep soundly knowing i’m not on Epstein’s list.
