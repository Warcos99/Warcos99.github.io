# Ceaseless Hunger 

### And the Cult of Capitalism

Oh Ceaseless Hunger take for
yourself the nutrients of the
earth and leave behind your
excrement for us to enjoy, as
the rotten stench of dilapidated
buildings permeates the
senses.

>``` In a desperate attempt to generate wealth, we have become landlords and in the process, houses have become investments. Those with enough capital purchase these homes and make them inaccessible to people. As of 2024, in this country, we have a homeless population of nearly 327,000 people[1]. In 2022 the Census Bureau stated that there were approximately 15.1 million vacant homes[2]. For every person without a place to live, displaced by the systems we have that keep them addicted and poor, there exists about 46 empty homes that they cannot use. Even if the majority of these homes are not safe to inhabit, if only 1/46th of them are functional, that still leaves this country with more empty houses than homeless people. ```

Oh Ceaseless Hunger, make us
in your image, so that our
appetites will never waiver.
May our generational labor
feed your incessant wants, so
that our production of comforts
may please you.

>``` Our billionaire class are seeing a booming economy as corporations become more and more profitable, all the while the general population sees increasing costs of living and the destruction of the middle class. The richest 0.01% of our population holds about 5% of the wealth[3], a number that is so much bigger than it should ever be! To top it off, most of this wealth comes from inheritances rather than entrepreneurship[4], so you can’t really say this is something that they have earned through hard work. Chris Kempczinski is the current CEO of McDonald's, and in 2023 he was compensated 1,212 times more than the median employee[5]. To put this into context, if the median employee at McDonald's got paid $14 an hour, then Chris would get paid about $17,000 an hour (enough to pay my rent for a whole year). In a single hour of work he would make enough to pay just under a year’s worth of rent. How do we consolidate this with the fact that most Americans are living month to month and are barely able to afford their rent? ```


Oh Ceaseless Hunger, strike
down those who may take our
land for they are non-believers
and they are dirty. The
outsiders who invade the land,
only bring with them malice
and pain, infecting us all with
their poisons and their death.

>``` Unlike what one might hear from conservative pundits, drugs like fentanyl are not coming in from illegal immigrants crossing our borders. These drugs are coming into our country through legal ports of entry and are smuggled inside unassuming vehicles[6].The money that fuels the drug economy comes from Chinese investors wanting to secure their wealth in American shores. Through a complex web of connections between Chinese investors, Chinese gangs, drug cartels, and drug dealers, money gets shuffled around in a way that benefits these parties[7]. All in all, the drug trade has very little to do with the illegal immigrants seeking asylum, and more to do with the billionaire class trying to secure their wealth. The border conversation is clearly a scapegoat for the billionaire funded politician to manipulate us into ignoring these real economic truths. So then, why is it so easy for us to fall for this manipulation? ```


Oh Ceaseless Hunger, our
deepest desire is to be like you,
to please you as you rule over
this land. You promise you will
come into our stomachs, and
the power and the wealth you
hold will trickle down onto our
mouths. For that, Ceaseless
Hunger, we love you.

>``` We let all these sins against humanity stand; the dehumanization of the homeless, the abuse of the laborer, the demonisation of the outsider, and many more, because we want to see ourselves as better than them. If we work hard enough, if we trust our billionaires enough, one day we will become billionaires too. That is just the natural cycle of wealth, right? At some point all the money that exists in the stratosphere will fall, and we will be able to catch it, then it will be our turn. But the truth is that these people with wealth are dragons, hoarding their mountains of gold, and doing everything they can to make sure they are the only ones that can enjoy it. We will never be like them, no matter how hard we try. There are other aspects of life where the cult of capitalism seeps in and manipulates perceptions. Transphobia, for profit prisons, engineering of dangerous social media, and so on. Though all of these issues are incredibly complex, it is possible to examine them from the lens of powerful dragons wanting to remain in power. The issues described in this essay are simply the issues that have affected me the most. ```
